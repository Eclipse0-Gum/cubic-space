Lunar Jumping Robot

Engineering vertical mobility systems for low-gravity environments through mechanical energy storage and autonomous reset mechanisms.

About The Project

The Lunar Jumping Robot simulates vertical mobility in low-gravity environments. A motor winds a compression spring which stores energy and releases it to produce lift.

Purpose

Jump-based locomotion allows robots to overcome rough lunar terrain more effectively than traditional wheeled systems.

Materials

  • High Torque Motor
  • Steel Compression Spring
  • Microcontroller
  • Battery System
  • Lightweight Structural Frame

Goal

Achieve controlled 6–8 foot vertical jumps with a fully autonomous spring reset mechanism.
